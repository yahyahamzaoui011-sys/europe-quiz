Travel Information

Spain: popular for beaches on the Mediterranean Sea, high mountains for hiking, and cooler Atlantic coasts. Barcelona and Madrid are expensive, but Andalusia and Galicia are cheaper. July and August are the hottest months.

Italy: known for food, fashion, and fast cars. The north has the Alps, cold weather, ski resorts, and rich creamy food. The center has mild weather and simple fresh food. The south is warm, relaxed, with seafood and fruit. Rich in art and history throughout.

Germany: south has forests, hills and mountains. North has flat land. Mild climate with warm summers and cold winters. Backpackers love the reliable bus and train system. Berlin and Munich are must-see cities mixing modern art and history.

Czech Republic: officially renamed Czechia in 2016, located in central Europe. Relaxed lifestyle, good mix of culture and modern life. Prague is the capital, split by the Vltava River into the Old Town (famous clock, lively squares) and the Lesser Town (quiet streets, historical buildings).

Poland: large country with Baltic Sea beaches in the north and Tatra Mountains in the south. Important WWII history. Cheaper than Western Europe but not the cheapest in the East. Mild climate like neighbouring Germany.
